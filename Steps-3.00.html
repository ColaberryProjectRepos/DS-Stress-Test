    Message from the General:  

    This stress test is all about the models the team has applied to the dataset after prepping the features. Once the team identifies which type of Machine Learning (supervised/unsupervised, regression/classification/clustering) they would apply to the dataset in order to answer their questions, they would be on the right path to choose the appropriate class of models. Various aspects of the stress test are

    • Modeling - The task of applying ML models to the dataset

    • Evaluation - The task of evaluating the performance of each model using an appropriate metric

    • Inference - The task of extracting predictions or insights from the data

    • Interpretation - The task of interpreting the meaning of the outcome and what it would mean from a business perspective

    In this stress test, the projectâ€™s core will be tested. What models were applied and what metric was chosen to evaluate various models? What model ultimately performed the best and would likely end up in deployment?

     

    What to critique:

    These are just some suggestions under the Modeling, Evaluation, Inference and Interpretation sections. Feel free to look for anything you feel appropriate for the dataset and the project in question and critique accordingly.

    Modeling: Did the team apply the appropriate class of models to the dataset for the given problem statement? Were the models coded accurately? Were there any errors or warnings and did the team address them?

    Model Evaluation: What metric did the team choose to evaluate the models and is this metric appropriate to use in the current use case? Did the student provide justification for why the specific metric was chosen?

    Inferences: Has the team passed appropriate test data or sample data to extract new predictions/labels/insights from the ML model? Are the insights generated by the students correct and appropriate?

    Interpretation: Has the team interpreted the results appropriately? Did they state what the model accuracy means and how it would affect predictions? Did they provide interpretation of how business would use this interpretation and what is the benefit of this insight? Does it make sense to you?
