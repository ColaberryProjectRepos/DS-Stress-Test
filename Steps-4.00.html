Message from the General:  

This critique is about Messaging.  This portion of the project will be what's on their BIO.  To get a better understanding, here is an example of a BIO and here is an example of a documented Project on hire.refactored.ai.

 

Note that the messaging should be reflected not just in the slides/presentation but in some level of detail in every piece of documentation. For example, the step-by-step notebook must begin with a problem statement and a proper message about the business problem we intend to solve with ML. The level of details would vary depending on the type of documentation, for example Slides would focus more on the messaging and business problem and less on code, architecture and modeling, whereas, step-by-step notebook will focus more on code, architecture and modeling and less on messaging or problem statement. But every document must have messaging.

 

What to critique: If you look at the Project documentation on hire.refactored.ai you will notice there is a certain format for the flow.  It is meant to be understood by everyone - not just technical people.

  • Problem Statement - The reason for the entire solution

  • Executive Summary - What was built and what troubles were faced

  • Data Dictionary - What was the understanding of the data and what features were used as predictors vs which was the target etc.

  • Key Insights & Take Aways - What did we learn

  • Suggestions / Next Steps - What do we do next
