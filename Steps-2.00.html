    Message from the General:  

    This stress test is all about the features the team selected for the model and how good their exploratory data analysis is (including visualizations) and their understanding of the dataset with relevance to the problem at hand.  Alumni / JRPers / Staff and Mentors,  I invite you all to take part in this process.  Take a few minutes out of your day to critique the projects circulated through the Center of Excellence Program.  Why?

    1. Purpose - Every project created in the workplace will be critiqued by the business.  It will then go through this back and forth process of change request and adjustments.  After continuous back and forth, the end result is something the business can feel good about using.  We are replicating that process here to ensure everything that flows through C.O.E. raises it's quality.  That's why it is a Center of Excellence.

    2. Rapid Experience Level - Never will you come in contact with as many projects as you will see come through the C.O.E. program.  You will see project after project getting transformed from something you wouldn't show your mom - into something you'd be willing to show off on social media.  Participating in that experience will give you years of experience in a very short period of time.

    3. Ideas and Insight - Watching projects get turned from zero to hero will give you tons of ideas you can use on your projects.  Go and look through past Message Board Critiques.  Head over to the List/ToDo section and access the ToDo Tickets to see the journey taken through each project.

     

    What to critique:

    These are just some suggestions under the Data Cleaning, Transformation and Visualization areas. Feel free to look for anything you feel appropriate for the dataset and the project in question and critique accordingly.

    Data Cleaning: Did the team look for missing values, NaN and 0 values in the features and were they addressed appropriately? Did the team look at any unvalidated characters in a specific way and made sure invalid observations are addressed appropriately? Did the team remove leading and trailing white spaces etc?

    Data Transformations: Are all features/columns converted into their appropriate data type based on the feature values? For example, numbers should be ‘int’ or ‘float’ and not ‘object’ type. Dates should be datetime type. Are categorical variables appropriately encoded and are ready to use in any ML model?

    Data Visualizations: The team must have at least 3 different visualizations for the dataset. They may choose to visualize individual features or all the features or a combination of some. The visualizations should be appropriately labeled, sized and appropriate in terms of what the student is trying to understand about the dataset.

    Refer to the DS project instructions and ensure that the students follow most if not every instruction and step which is appropriate for this project.
