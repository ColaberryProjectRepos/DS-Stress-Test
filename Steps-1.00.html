    Message from the General:  

    There are two components to setting up the problem statement for any Data Science Project - the objective of the study and the data required to perform such a study (or build the ML model). In the real world, the process may start with either one of these two components as shown below.

    1. You or the stakeholders in your organization may have a specific objective in mind, a question you may want to answer and then you would go on a hunt to acquire datasets which can help you answer your questions.

    2. You may have a dataset which the stakeholders present to you and you explore the dataset to identify what question you may answer by applying an ML model to it. Not all problems need an ML model, so it is extremely important to justify why ML is an appropriate solution.

    For our purpose the team would have picked a dataset first and then would try to come up with a use case where an ML model may be applied to the dataset in order to solve some real world business problem. This is the crux of the problem statement.

    What to critique:

    Problem Statement: Does the team understand what problem they are trying to solve and have they articulated it appropriately? Have they listed the source of their dataset(s), the background or origins of the dataset and how it would help them answer the question they would like to answer? Does their business question require a Supervised or Unsupervised model? If it is supervised, would it be a regression model or a classification model? What are some industry and ML domain labels that the team has assigned to the project and are they appropriate?

    Data Acquisition: What are all the sources of data the team has used and have they provided links and a brief description of these datasets and what method did they use to acquire the datasets? Is it appropriate?

    Data Dictionary: The team must provide a valid data dictionary with all required columns and it should have no mistakes in terms of datatypes of each column, description of these columns and units of the values in each column etc.

    Refer to the questions in DS steps to identify all questions that the team needs to answer and cover, up to the step including Data Dictionary (step 3).

     

    Message to the team: Never stop moving forward.  You are to continue to multitask and let nothing stop your progress.  The stress test may take a couple of days but that is no reason why you can't start exploring your data.  The stress test will produce either validation or cause you to alter your design, but either way it will save you tons of time in the grand scheme of things.
